내가 좋아하는 한국의 산 TOP10

1. 지리산(천왕봉, 해발 1915m)

지리산은 지리산은 경상남도, 전라남도, 전북특별자치도에 걸쳐 있는 산이다. 주봉인 천왕봉 외에 반야봉, 연화봉, 바래봉을 비롯하여 뱀사골, 피아골, 한신계곡이 유명하다. 지리산은 4계절 모두 아름다워 연간 300만명이 찾는다.

K2

K2 is the second-highest mountain in the world, located on the China–Pakistan border. It is known for its extreme difficulty and has the second-highest fatality rate among the eight-thousanders.

Kangchenjunga

Kangchenjunga is the third-highest mountain in the world. It lies between Nepal and Sikkim, India, with three of the five peaks directly on the border, and the remaining two in Nepal's Taplejung District.

Lhotse

Lhotse is the fourth-highest mountain in the world, located at the border between Tibet (China) and the Khumbu region of Nepal. It is connected to Mount Everest via the South Col.

Makalu

Makalu is the fifth-highest mountain in the world, located in the Mahalangur range of the Nepal Himalayas, southeast of Mount Everest. It is an isolated peak whose shape is a four-sided pyramid.

Cho Oyu

Cho Oyu is the sixth-highest mountain in the world, located a short distance from Mount Everest in the Himalayas. The mountain stands on the Tibet-Nepal border.

Dhaulagiri

Dhaulagiri is the seventh-highest mountain in the world, located in Nepal. It is one of the most formidable mountains to climb and has a distinctive pyramid shape.

Manaslu

Manaslu is the eighth-highest mountain in the world, located in the Nepalese Himalayas. The name Manaslu means "mountain of the spirit," and it was first climbed by Toshio Imanishi and Gyalzen Norbu in 1956.

Nanga Parbat

Nanga Parbat is the ninth-highest mountain in the world, located in the Diamer District of Pakistan's Gilgit-Baltistan region. It is known as the "Killer Mountain" due to its high fatality rate.

Annapurna

Annapurna is the tenth-highest mountain in the world, located in Nepal. It is renowned for its challenging climbing routes and has one of the highest fatality rates among the eight-thousanders.
